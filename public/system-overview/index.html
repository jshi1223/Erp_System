Executive Summary

This document defines the core business processes supported by the Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system for Kinaadman IT Solutions, Inc. The named organization provides both hardware goods and professional services including installation and maintenance, requiring a complex workflow management system.

The ERP system is designed to streamline operations across sales, procurement, inventory, service delivery, and financial management, ensuring seamless coordination between departments and accurate tracking of all transactions.

System Overview

Core Modules

  • ERP Security Module (User Account Creation, Role-Based Access Control, Authentications, Audit & Reviews)
  • Sales Management (Goods & Services)
  • Service Operations (Installation, Maintenance, Support)
  • Procurement Management
  • Inventory Management
  • Financial Management (AP, AR, GL)
  • Customer Relationship Management

Implemented ERP System

Core Module System Status Implemented Scope
ERP Security Module Implemented User registration, email verification, account approval, login sessions, role-based access, audit logs, and admin/user management.
Sales Management Implemented Customer billing, sales invoices, project sales records, collections, customer balances, and receivable tracking through the Sales Management module and AR finance views.
Service Operations Implemented Service orders for installation, maintenance, support, project linkage, status monitoring, and service billing source records.
Procurement Management Implemented Vendor directory, purchase requisitions, RFQ, quotations, purchase orders, goods receipts, approval workflow, and supporting PDFs.
Inventory Management Implemented Product master, warehouse master, stock on hand, stock in/out adjustments, low-stock monitoring, and recent inventory movement history.
Financial Management Implemented Accounts Payable, Accounts Receivable, payments, billing, collections, project ledger summaries, dashboards, and financial reports.
Customer Relationship Management Partially Implemented Company registry, customer/project profiles, service history, billing records, receivable balances, and project communication trail.

Module Relationships

  • Security to All Modules: user role and approval status control access to admin, finance, procurement, reports, and user pages.
  • Master Data to All Operations: company, customer, vendor, and business entity records become the source reference for sales, service, project, procurement, inventory, and finance documents.
  • Sales to Service Operations: customer sales and billing requirements can be linked to service orders for installation, maintenance, and support delivery.
  • Service Operations to Projects: service orders can be tied to project records so work scope, schedules, responsible teams, and project ledgers stay traceable.
  • Sales/Service to AR: sales invoices, project transactions, and service orders create customer receivables, collections, balances, and aging views.
  • Projects to Procurement/AP: project requirements create PR, RFQ, quotation, PO, receiving, supplier bills, and supplier payments.
  • Procurement to Inventory: received goods can be reflected as stock movements for product and warehouse tracking.
  • Procurement to Financial Management: received goods/services and supplier documents flow into Accounts Payable.
  • Financial Management to Reports: AP, AR, payments, net position, and ledger data feed the dashboard and management reports.
  • Reports to All Modules: reports summarize project, procurement, AP, AR, payment, and audit information for management review.
